What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to was the underlined headings. I believed that pressing them would link me to a different part of the website

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Scrolling down
  • Clicking the text
  • Clicking the gifs
  • Highlighting the text
  • Clicking the grid, which turned out to be doors
  • Clicking and dragging the cakes
  • Stacking the cakes together
  • Clicking the back button often

    • What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

    I spent the most time dragging around the cupcakes and decor hidden on the left side of the home page

    What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

    My most common interaction on the website was clicking around to see if any other surprises were hidden

    What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

    My impression of the interactive experience’s intended goal was to surprise me with hidden elements and make me check if there are other interactions in the website.

    How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

    The website design was very low-key; it was black and white and lacked any interesting interaction at a surface level. To hint at a surprise interaction, the website laid clues in the articles the designer chose to display. Once the user puts the pieces together and finds out the hidden secret, they would be rewarded with a surprise interaction.

    What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

    I believe that the cupcakes and decor is the surprise that the user gets after interacting with the website for a while. The user would first skim through all of the website’s pages and click on the text and images. After taking a closer look, the user would realize that there are cupcakes hidden behind doors disguised as grids. After finding the surprise, the user would go through everything a second time to check if they had missed anything.

    How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

    The interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with by presenting itself as very low-key, laying out clues that hint at something more special. After the special interaction is found, the user would want to go through the website a second time to see if they have missed anything.

    What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

    The experience references other projects, brands, experiences, and their website’s personal interactivity.

    What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

    The references communicate to me that there is more than meets the eye, and hint at me to pay more attention to the details.

    What does this reference communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

    I believe that the reference is communicating to me that I should be more humble, as my initial impression of this website was that the designer made something uninspiring and amateurish. After I realized I missed something about the website, I felt a little foolish.

    What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

    The most frustrating part about the interaction are the links that take you to another tab. There are links that take you to different areas of the website, and others that take you to a different website. Both of these links look the same, and I find it annoying when I am unknowingly taken to another tab.

    What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

    I find the most satisfying part of the interaction to be finding the cupcakes and realizing the references in the website were hinting at something hidden the whole time. It feels like watching the movie for a second time and finding all the hidden details.
